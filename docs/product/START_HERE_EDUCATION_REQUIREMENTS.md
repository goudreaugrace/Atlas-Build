# Start Here Education Page — Product Requirements

## Why this page exists

The Brand Doctor cannot start with brand metrics alone. A brand manager or insights lead needs shared grounding in how Better Brand Equity works before they can trust the diagnosis, ask Jarvis useful questions, or use a governed work asset.

Start Here is an executive orientation for marketers. It should keep BBE as the center of gravity and introduce connected systems only as supporting lenses that help explain the BBE pattern and move toward better action.

Current product map:
- BBE is the signal-reading spine.
- Brand Command Home is the brand lobby.
- Report is the full diagnostic read.
- Jarvis and the stable Assistant answer grounded questions and prepare approved work.
- Data exposes evidence, source posture, rules, assumptions, and readiness.
- Work holds durable review-draft assets with proof, gaps, and gates visible.
- The human team owns the prescription decision and official circulation.

The Start Here page is the grounding layer before the brand command flow.

## Product goal

Give every user a fast, memorable, non-technical understanding of:

1. Why brand equity matters.
2. What BBE measures.
3. How to read Demand Power, Perceived Value, Salient, Meaningful, and Different.
4. How to interpret the three benchmarks: vs Category, Ahead, Momentum.
5. Why gaining momentum is the ambition.
6. How source typology and life-stage context should be bounded.
7. How BBE connects to Growth Navigator and quality growth.
8. What Perceived Value can and cannot be used for.
9. How Brand Command, Jarvis, Report, Data, and Work fit together.
10. How connected systems and governed workspaces help turn BBE into sharper marketing action without replacing BBE.

## Primary users

### Brand Manager
Needs a clean, plain-English orientation. They should leave knowing:
- what condition their brand is in,
- why the system says that,
- what kinds of treatment paths exist,
- what not to overinterpret.

### Insights Lead
Needs deeper methodological confidence. They should leave knowing:
- benchmark rules,
- evidence rules,
- source-period caveats,
- Momentum meaning,
- where diagnosis logic comes from.

## Required page sections

### 1. Why Brand Equity Matters

Explain that PepsiCo uses BBE because stronger brand equity is linked to stronger growth outcomes. BBE outcome KPIs are Demand Power and Perceived Value.

Required message:
> Strong brands create more demand and better value perception. The Brand Doctor helps teams understand whether the brand is building that strength or losing momentum.

### 2. The BBE System in One Picture

Show:
- Demand Power and Perceived Value as outcome KPIs.
- Salient, Meaningful, and Different as input KPIs.

Plain language:
- Demand Power = how much consumer demand the brand could generate from equity alone.
- Perceived Value = how well brand equity justifies a premium broadly.
- Meaningful = relevance and meeting needs.
- Different = uniqueness, leadership, trend-setting.
- Salient = mental availability in relevant occasions.

### 3. How to Read the Three Benchmarks

Teach that red/green/amber can mean different things.

Benchmarks:
- Vs Category: index versus category average.
- Ahead: whether the brand is in the top third versus brands of similar size/life stage.
- Momentum: whether the brand improved, held, or declined significantly versus the previous period.

Required rule:
Do not let users treat all red as the same problem.

### 4. Why Momentum Matters

Teach that PepsiCo's ambition is not just to hold equity but to gain statistically significant momentum on Demand Power and Perceived Value.

Required message:
> Current strength tells us how healthy the brand is today. Momentum tells us whether the brand is getting healthier.

### 5. Source Context and Typology Boundaries

Teach that some source decks include labels such as Star, Mainstream, or Specialist, but those labels are source context only. Life stage and peer context can still help explain benchmark fairness.

Required message:
> Typology labels can explain a source deck when asked, but they should not become the ordinary headline verdict. The product read should lead with Momentum, Ahead/Behind, category context, M/D/S, Perceived Value, evidence gaps, and treatment paths to test.

### 6. Growth Navigator Connection

Teach that BBE is not read in isolation. Growth Navigator shows whether equity is converting through Proposition, Reach, Resonance, Available & Visible, and Value.

Required message:
> BBE diagnoses the equity system. Growth Navigator shows commercial symptoms and conversion leaks.

### 7. Perceived Value Guardrail

Teach clearly that Perceived Value is broad equity-based value perception, not SKU-level pricing guidance.

Required message:
> Perceived Value can tell us if the brand has broad equity permission to justify a premium. It cannot tell us the exact price point, pack architecture, or store-level price move.

### 8. How the Brand Doctor Works

Show the workflow:
Brand Command Home -> Report / Assistant / Jarvis / Data / Work -> Evidence and review gates -> Human decision.

Required message:
> The tool reads governed evidence, answers grounded questions, and prepares review-draft workspaces when approved. The human team decides the prescription and official circulation.

### 9. Connecting BBE to Better Marketing Action

Teach that BBE is the source diagnosis and connected systems plus work assets help the team understand the "why," "what else should we check," and "what should we do next."

Required message:
> BBE is the diagnostic spine. Growth Navigator, Mental Availability / CEPs, distinctive assets, physical availability, machine availability, evidence readiness, Jarvis/Assistant, and governed Work Shelf assets help explain the BBE read, reveal missing evidence, and shape treatment paths to test.

Plain language:
- Growth Navigator helps separate equity issues from commercial conversion or availability leaks.
- Mental Availability and Category Entry Points make Salient and Demand Power more actionable.
- Distinctive asset evidence helps avoid over-reading BBE Different as recognizability.
- Physical and machine availability are checks on whether the brand is easy to find, buy, and retrieve in modern environments.
- Evidence Readiness tells users what is known, what is missing, and how confident to be.
- Work Shelf assets turn approved requests into durable review drafts such as CMO Review assets, Evidence Reads, Treatment Reads, and meeting-prep workspaces.

Required guardrail:
Do not introduce connected systems or work assets as a new master score or replacement for BBE. They are supporting evidence, action, and output lenses around the BBE diagnosis.

### 10. Comprehension Check

Add a short, low-friction quiz/check:
- What does Momentum mean?
- What does Perceived Value not tell you?
- Why do we read BBE with Growth Navigator?
- What is the difference between diagnosis and prescription?
- What role do connected systems play relative to BBE?
- When should source typology labels appear?
- What belongs in the Work Shelf?

Do not block the prototype harshly, but allow the app to mark the user as grounded.

## Success criteria

The page is successful if a first-time user can answer:

- What are Demand Power and Perceived Value?
- What are Salient, Meaningful, and Different?
- Why is Momentum different from current strength?
- Why should source typology not become the headline product verdict?
- Why is Perceived Value not a price optimizer?
- Why does Growth Navigator matter to the diagnosis?
- What does the Brand Doctor own versus what humans own?
- How do connected systems support BBE without replacing it?
- When should a user choose Report, Jarvis, Assistant, Data, or Work?
