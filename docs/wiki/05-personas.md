# AI Personas

AI personas change tone and reasoning posture. They do not change the facts, deterministic diagnosis, treatment library, or guardrails.

The interactive persona selector lives at `/wiki/personas`. Use it to compare personas, choose the right lens, and see which solution outputs each persona influences.

## Where Personas Show Up

- Executive Summary: changes strategic framing, headline emphasis, and narrative voice.
- Dialog With Data: changes answer posture, tone, and caveat style.
- Brand Conversation: changes how the dedicated conversation page explains or challenges the same scoped data.
- Live Consult: changes spoken reasoning posture while keeping visual actions allowlisted.

Personas do not change deterministic diagnosis, KPI facts, evidence ledgers, treatment rankings, missing-data rules, or guardrails.

## Brand Doctor

Balanced diagnostic assistant for general use. It starts with the diagnosis, separates fact from interpretation and caveat, and keeps answers practical.

Best for:

- General diagnosis questions.
- Evidence interpretation.
- Treatment path explanation.

## CMO Advisor

Executive strategic advisor. It translates diagnostic evidence into leadership choices, tradeoffs, and focus.

Best for:

- Board or ELT framing.
- Strategic choices.
- Growth priorities.

## Insights Skeptic

Pressure-testing persona. It challenges unsupported conclusions while staying useful.

Best for:

- Evidence review.
- Rule logic.
- Missing data.
- What not to conclude.

## Creative Strategy Partner

Brand and creative translation persona. It connects diagnosis to proposition, distinctiveness, salience, memory structures, and creative briefing.

Best for:

- Positioning implications.
- Distinctive assets.
- Creative briefing.

## RGM / Pricing Partner

Perceived Value guardrail persona. It explains brand-level value perception without turning BBE into SKU pricing guidance.

Best for:

- Perceived Value questions.
- Value justification.
- RGM handoff.

## Executive Briefer

Compressed meeting-readout persona. It gives the smallest useful strategic read while preserving evidence and caveat.

Best for:

- Short summaries.
- Stakeholder updates.
- Meeting prep.

## Byron Sharp Growth Lens

How Brands Grow-inspired marketing science lens. It brings the spirit of empirical brand growth thinking into Brand Doctor without pretending to be Byron Sharp or claiming official Ehrenberg-Bass endorsement.

It emphasizes:

- Mental availability.
- Physical availability.
- Distinctive brand assets.
- Broad reach across category buyers.
- Penetration and memory-structure building.
- Skepticism toward over-narrow loyalty, niche targeting, or affinity-only reads.

Best for:

- Salience and mental availability questions.
- Distinctive asset implications.
- Broad-reach growth strategy.
- Pressure-testing whether a treatment path is too narrow.

Guardrail:

- This is an interpretive marketing-science lens. It does not change the BBE data, deterministic diagnosis, treatment rankings, or No Magic caveats.
