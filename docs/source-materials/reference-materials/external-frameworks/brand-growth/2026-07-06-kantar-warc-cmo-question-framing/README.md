# Kantar / WARC Brand Growth Question-Framing References — 2026-07-06

## What This Is

This folder documents live external framework references reviewed while building the governed `provocation_questions` module.

These are not PepsiCo source records and are not brand-specific evidence. They are useful for shaping executive question types, learning content, and reusable domain-shell design.

## Sources Reviewed

### Kantar Meaningful Different Salient Framework

- URL: https://www.kantar.com/inspiration/brands/what-is-the-meaningful-different-salient-framework
- Published: 2022-07-18
- Relevant learning:
  - MDS measures brand equity accumulated in consumers' minds.
  - Kantar frames MDS as connected to penetration, market share, willingness to pay, and future growth potential.
- Product use:
  - Supports the rule that Meaningful, Different, Salient, Demand Power, and Perceived Value should be interpreted together.
  - Supports CMO-level questions that connect brand equity to growth while preserving evidence caveats.

### Kantar Blueprint For Brand Growth

- URL: https://www.kantar.com/campaigns/blueprint-for-brand-growth
- Reviewed: 2026-07-06
- Relevant learning:
  - The Blueprint frames a growth driver, "Be Meaningfully Different to More People," and three growth accelerators: Predispose More People, Be More Present, and Find New Space.
  - Kantar describes the framework as built from attitudinal and behavioral data at scale.
- Product use:
  - Supports executive question framing around whether the brand needs to predispose more people, be more present, or find new space.
  - Does not replace PepsiCo source data, BBE data, Growth Navigator data, or brand strategy context.

### Kantar BrandZ Most Valuable Global Brands 2026

- URL: https://www.kantar.com/campaigns/brandz/global
- Published context: 2026 Global BrandZ page
- Relevant learning:
  - The 2026 page frames winning brands as Meaningfully Different at every touchpoint.
  - It names a Chief Marketing Objectives framework that helps focus whether to fix fundamentals, accelerate momentum, or find new space.
  - It describes BrandZ brand value methodology with Demand Power, Pricing Power, and Future Power.
- Product use:
  - Supports the `provocation_questions` module's CMO-level question: "Where should leadership focus first: fix fundamentals, accelerate momentum, or find new space?"
  - Supports educational context around Demand Power / Pricing Power lineage.

### WARC The Multiplier Effect

- URL: https://page.warc.com/the-multiplier-effect-report
- Reviewed: 2026-07-06
- Relevant learning:
  - WARC frames a gap between brand and performance silos.
  - The report page argues that brand equity can act as an accelerant for commercial performance when integrated with performance tactics.
- Product use:
  - Supports executive question framing that bridges brand equity and commercial performance.
  - Reinforces the guardrail that Brand Doctor should not collapse brand equity reads into short-term tactics, SKU-level pricing, or unsupported causal claims.

## Product Translation

The `provocation_questions` module uses these references as **question framing**, not evidence:

- Kantar MDS -> ask how M/D/S and Perceived Value jointly explain the read.
- Kantar Blueprint -> ask whether the next growth question is predisposition, presence, or new space.
- Kantar BrandZ Chief Marketing Objectives -> ask whether leadership should fix fundamentals, accelerate momentum, or find new space.
- WARC Multiplier Effect -> ask how to connect brand equity to commercial performance without losing the brand-building logic.

- evidence to use
- evidence needed to answer
