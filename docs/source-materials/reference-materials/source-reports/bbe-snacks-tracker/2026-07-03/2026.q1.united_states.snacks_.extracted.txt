Pricing Power is the ability of a brand to justify a price premium relative to the category average, based purely on perceptions, absent of activation factors
Note: Above average (>=1.10); Average (0.90<x<1.10); Below average (<=0.90) 
Q.PERCEIVED PRICE: Drag each brand onto the scale to show how much you think it costs. 7(Costs the most)  6  5  4  3  2  1(Costs the least)
Q.PERCEIVED WORTH: Is this brand worth more or less than it costs? Worth more | Worth the same | Worth less 
The Pricing Power score is only intended to be about the role of brand equity in justifying a premium
It does not tell you about how specific product types, individual SKUs, the packaging, the stores in which a brand is stocked, the on-shelf display and the competitive context in each store might determine the price people are willing to pay. These can often be massive influences
Furthermore, the Pricing Power score is very intentionally about the overall brand and the price that the equity of that overall brand can support, rather than being about specific pricing of individual products
For these reasons, the Pricing Power score should only be used to give broad pricing indications
It should not be used to suggest specific price-points for specific products. This can only be derived from more detailed pricing studies using techniques such as Conjoint/Trade-Off, Newton Miller Smith or Market Mix Modelling. Please contact your local Data Science team for more information on these techniques if required

## Slide 27: STOP. NOTE: Pricing Power is just one piece of a larger complicated puzzle around pricing strategies
STOP. NOTE: Pricing Power is just one piece of a larger complicated puzzle around pricing strategies

Purpose

How to use

Piece of Puzzle

The Pricing Power score is only intended to be about the role of brand equity in justifying a premium
The Pricing Power score is very intentionally about the overall brand, and the price that the equity of that overall brand can support

There are other massive influences on the price people are willing to pay that are not accounted for in Pricing Power:
Specific pricing of individual products / SKUs
Packaging
The stores in which a brand is stocked
The on-shelf display
The competitive context in each store

For these reasons, the Pricing Power score should only be used to give broad pricing indications

Specific price-points for specific products can only be derived from more detailed pricing studies using techniques such as Conjoint/Trade-Off, Newton Miller Smith or Market Mix Modelling. Please contact your local Data Science team for more information on these techniques if required

Smaller brands often need to “act big” to build Salience

Identify the main competitor’s weakness and strategize against that:
When a competitor’s vulnerability is in Difference, then it will be worthwhile to put laser focus on this to compete
When a competitor’s vulnerability is in Salience, it’s worth outshouting them with high Share of Voice behind high-quality creative, and pushing physical availability levers to be more present than they are
When a competitor’s vulnerability is in Meaningful, then it’s worth delivering the best Experience and connecting with consumers to outperform them
